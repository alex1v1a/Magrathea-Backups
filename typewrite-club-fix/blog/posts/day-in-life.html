A Day in the Life: Running an App Company from Auckland

The reality of remote work and building products from the edge of the world.

Paper Trail Team Founders

People often ask what it's like running an app company from New Zealand. The short answer: it's different, but in mostly good ways. Here's a look at a typical day building Paper Trail from Auckland.

6:30 AM - The Morning Ritual

The day starts early. Partly because we're morning people, partly because time zones are a thing. When it's 7 AM in Auckland, it's still yesterday in San Francisco. The overlap with US customers and partners is limited, so we front-load our synchronous communication.

Coffee is non-negotiable. New Zealand takes its coffee seriously, and we've become particular about our flat whites. The local café knows our order. Small joys matter when you're grinding on a startup.

8:00 AM - Deep Work Block

Mornings are for deep work. No meetings, no Slack, just code and writing. The world is quiet. The US is asleep. We can focus without the constant ping of notifications.

This is when the hard problems get solved. Architecture decisions. Complex bug fixes. Strategic planning. The four hours between 8 AM and noon are consistently the most productive of the day.

12:00 PM - Break and Reset

Lunch is a hard stop. We step away from screens, often walking to the beach if the weather cooperates. Auckland's harbor is hard to beat for a midday reset. The water, the volcanic peaks, the weirdly changeable weather—it all helps clear the head.

1:00 PM - The Overlap Window

Afternoons are for collaboration. This is when we overlap with US West Coast mornings (5-6 PM NZ time). We batch meetings, customer calls, and real-time discussions into this window.

The time zone difference is a double-edged sword. On one hand, we have limited overlap. On the other, we get uninterrupted mornings. After years of this schedule, we wouldn't trade it.

4:00 PM - Review and Ship

Late afternoon is for shipping. We review code, run final tests, and deploy. There's something satisfying about pushing updates while the day is still young enough to monitor for issues.

5:00 PM - Switch Off

We try to respect boundaries. Work ends at 5, give or take. The laptop closes. Evening is for family, friends, and the excellent Auckland restaurant scene.

The Trade-offs

Running a global company from New Zealand isn't without challenges:

  • Travel is brutal. Any business trip involves long flights. We've learned to batch trips and work from the air.
  • Customer support is asynchronous. Most support happens via email and chat, not calls. This works for us, but it's different.
  • Funding is harder. The NZ venture scene is small. We raised from international investors, which meant extra travel and complexity.

But the benefits outweigh the costs:

  • Quality of life is exceptional. Work-life balance isn't just a phrase here; it's the default.
  • Cost efficiency. We can stretch funding further than Bay Area competitors.
  • Different perspective. Being outside the Silicon Valley bubble gives us a different lens on product and markets.

The Bottom Line

You don't need to be in San Francisco to build a tech company anymore. You need good internet, a solid team, and the discipline to make time zones work for you, not against you. From where we sit—looking out at the harbor on a good day—the future feels bright.
