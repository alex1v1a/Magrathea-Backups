Customer Story: The Big Catch

John had been fishing the same spots for 20 years. Then he found Reel Reviews, and everything changed.

The Skeptic

"I don't trust apps," John told us when we first met him at the Auckland Boat Show. "They're all written by people who don't actually fish."

But something about Reel Reviews caught his attention. Maybe it was the negative reviews—honest complaints from real anglers about what went wrong. Or maybe it was just curiosity.

The Discovery

John downloaded the app on a Tuesday. By Thursday, he'd found a spot he'd never heard of—a small bay on Waiheke Island with terrible reviews.

"The reviews said the fish weren't biting, the wind was bad, and the rocks were slippery. But they also said the snapper were huge when conditions were right. I checked the weather forecast and saw a window."

The Trip

John drove to the ferry, crossed to Waiheke, and hiked down to the bay. It was exactly as described—rocky, windy, and completely empty. Perfect.

He cast his line at 6 AM. By 8 AM, he'd caught three snapper, the biggest weighing 8 pounds. His personal best.

The Review

John posted his first review that evening. Five stars? No. He gave it three stars—honest about the difficult access and weather sensitivity. But he also noted the fish size and recommended the early morning window.

"That's the thing about Reel Reviews," he told us later. "It's not about finding perfect spots. It's about finding the right spot for the right day. The negative reviews tell you more than the positive ones ever could."

The Result

John has been using Reel Reviews for six months now. He's discovered four new spots, avoided three overfished areas, and caught more fish than ever.

He's also become one of our most active reviewers, posting honest, detailed reports that help other anglers make informed decisions.

"I was skeptical. Now I tell every fisherman I know to download it. Just don't tell them about my spots."
